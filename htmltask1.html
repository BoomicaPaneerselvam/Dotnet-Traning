WHAT IS PANEER BUTTER MASALA?

Paneer butter masala also known as butter paneer is a thick, rich and creamy tomato based curry in which paneer cubes is fried and soaked. This curry is little sweet and not that spicy at all.This is a popular recipe in any restaurants similar to kadai paneer, shahi paneer, palak paneer and malai kofta.

ABOUT CREAMY RESTAURANT STYLE PANEER BUTTER MASALA!

  • Paneer butter masala is favourite recipe of many people. It may be butter chicken for non veg lovers, but paneer butter masala has a special place in many peoples heart.
  • This is my favourite recipe for paneer butter masala. I make it very often for my family. It taste amazing and is easy to make as well. Making butter paneer is pretty easy too. First make a paste out of onions and tomatoes. Then fry cubed paneer in oil till golden.
  • Now prepare a masala in butter. Sizzle some cumin seeds and cook the onion tomato masala along with spice powders, salt and sugar. Cook till oil separates. Add paneer cubes and toss well in the masala. Add in cream, kasuri methi leaves and coriander leaves.
  • Rich, thick, creamy butter paneer is ready to serve with some hot naan or parathas. It is very simple to make it in instant pot.
Paneer Butter Masala | Paneer Butter Masala Recipe

INGREDIENTS

  • 200 grams Paneer
  • 4 Tomato chopped
  • 1 Onion chopped
  • 10 Cashews
  • 1 tbsp Oil
  • 1 tsp Shahi jeera
  • 2 tsp Ginger garlic paste
  • tbsp Kashmiri chilli powder
  • 2 tsp Cumin powder
  • 1 tsp Garam masala powder
  • 1 tbsp Sugar
  • Salt to taste
  • ¼ cup Cream
  • 1 tbsp Kasuri methi leaves
  • 2 tbsp Butter
  • Coriander leaves
INSTRUCTIONS
  1. oil in a pan. Add in onions and saute for 2 mins.
  2. Add in cashews and mix well. Add in tomatoes and cook till mushy. take this in a blender and puree till smooth.
  3. Heat 1 tbsp butter and Add in cumin seeds, ginger garlic paste and let them sizzle.
  4. Add in pureed onion tomato masala and saute till oil separates.
  5. Add in spice powders, salt, sugar and mix well.
  6. Add in paneer and water and bring it to a boil. Simmer for 5 mins.
  7. Add in fresh cream, kasuri methi leaves and coriander leaves. Mix well.
  8. Add in 1 tbsp butter and mix well and Serve
NOTES
If the curd which you add curdles, take a hand blender or pour this into a blender and whiz it for few times and transfer it to the same kadai and continue with the process.
Cut the paneer into small pieces so that it absorbs all the masala well
You can entirely skip the addition of onion in this recipe. But I love the flavour of onions.
Instead of paneer you can use tofu as well.
Make sure you cook the onions and tomatoes till it is well cooked. It enhances the flavour.
You can chop the onions very finely and saute them first instead of grinding with tomatoes.
You can use store bought tomato puree instead of fresh tomatoes.
If you are using fresh tomatoes, use ripe tomatoes for the sweetness it adds to the curry. Also it adds red colour.
Make sure you use kashmiri chilli powder instead of normal chilli powder. Kashmiri chilli powder adds red colour.
If you don't want to use too much butter in this curry, start by cooking the curry in oil and add a knob of butter in the end
I prefer using unsalted butter in the curry. You could use any butter you prefer.
Traditionally white butter (makhan) is used. When grinding onions and tomatoes. Make sure you grind it super smooth.
Add green chillies if you like to have a bite of it in the curry. it adds flavours as well as little kick.
Instead of cream, You can use milk which is mixed with little cornstarch for a little thickness.
Don't be stingy in kasuri methi leaves. It adds the aroma which a paneer butter masala needs.


Serving Suggestions

Paneer butter masala is best served with rice, pulao and any indian bread.
